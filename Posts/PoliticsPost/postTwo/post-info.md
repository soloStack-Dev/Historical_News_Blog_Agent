

# Historical Politics: The French Revolution (1789)

The French Revolution began in 1789 and became one of the most significant political revolutions in world history. It ended centuries of absolute monarchy in France and introduced ideas that continue to influence democracies worldwide.

# Overview
- Country: France
- Period: 1789–1799
- Main Opponents: The French monarchy vs. revolutionaries
- Key Goal: End absolute monarchy and establish a government based on liberty, equality, and popular sovereignty.
Background

# Before the revolution, France faced severe problems:

- Heavy national debt after years of war.
- High taxes on ordinary citizens, while many nobles and clergy enjoyed exemptions.
- Food shortages and rising bread prices.
- Widespread dissatisfaction with the absolute rule of King Louis XVI.

# These economic and political pressures led to growing unrest among the people.

# Major Events
1. Storming of the Bastille (14 July 1789)

Citizens of Paris attacked the Bastille prison, a symbol of royal authority.

This event is widely regarded as the beginning of the French Revolution.

2. Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen

# The National Assembly adopted a declaration proclaiming principles such as:

- Equality before the law
- Freedom of speech
- Protection of property
- National sovereignty

# These ideas became foundational for many later democratic constitutions.

3. End of the Monarchy

- In 1792, France abolished the monarchy and became a republic.

- King Louis XVI was later tried for treason and executed in 1793.

4. The Reign of Terror

- Between 1793 and 1794, the revolutionary government executed thousands of people accused of opposing the revolution.

# This period demonstrated how political revolutions can also lead to instability and authoritarian measures.

5. Rise of Napoleon Bonaparte

- In 1799, Napoleon Bonaparte seized power, bringing the revolutionary period to an end while preserving many of its legal and administrative reforms.

# Political Significance

# The French Revolution transformed political thought by promoting:

Popular sovereignty
Constitutional government
Equal citizenship
Secular government
Human rights

# Its political influence spread far beyond France, inspiring political reform and independence movements across Europe and the Americas.

Interesting Facts
Bastille Day (14 July) remains France's national holiday.
The revolution introduced the motto: "Liberty, Equality, Fraternity."
Many modern democratic ideals trace part of their development to this period.
